  The National Telecommunications and Information Administration  
As it pertains to U.S. Code Title 47: Telecommunications

A Brief History

  The National Telecommunications and Information Administration (NTIA) came into being in 1978 as a result of restructuring an organization from the Executive Branch and the Commerce Department.

  This reorganization was included in Reorganization Plan Number 1 in 1977 and put into place by Executive Order 12046 in 1978. This executive order combined the roles and responsibilities of the Office of Telecommunications Policy (OTP) from the White House and the Office of Telecommunications which was previously under the Commerce Department.

  This reorganization essentially transferred the power held by the Executive Branch to the Commerce Department making the Assistant Secretary of Commerce the President’s chief advisor in matters of telecommunications both domestically and internationally.

Responsibilities and Functions:
  • Develop and award grant programs that continue to expand and strengthen the use of broadband and other communication technologies in the US.

  • Create policies related to online privacy, copyright protection, the economy of the Internet, Cybersecurity, and ensure the free and open sharing of information across a global Internet.

  • Managing and maintaining the range of radio waves used by the US for communications, including: FM and AM frequencies, as well as Wi-Fi and Bluetooth signal ranges.

  • The NTIA is also responsible for identifying new spectrum ranges that can be allocated for commercial use.

  • Ensuring and safeguarding the Internet’s DNS by coordinating with other agencies and organizations such as ICANN.

  • On the front line of researching and developing break through technologies for the Federal government as well as the private sectors.

NTIA Initiatives:

Offices of the NTIA
  The NTIA is divided into several offices which each have certain responsibilities in developing telecommunication policies in the US as well as coordinating efforts with international partners to maintain the operations and safety of telecommunication resources.

These offices include:

  • Office of the Assistant Secretary:

  • Assists the Assistant Secretary of Commerce in advising the Executive Branch in matters of telecommunication and information policies.

  • Office of Spectrum Management:

  • Maintains and secures the operations of the radio frequencies utilized by the Federal government.

  • Office of Internet Connectivity and Growth:

  • Strives to expand the required infrastructure for the Internet to ensure “digital equity” and deliver nation-wide accessibility to broadband.

  • Office of International Affairs:

  • Advises the President on international affairs pertaining to information and telecommunication policies.

  • Office of Policy Analysis and Development:

  • Is key in helping the agency advise the president on policies concerning developing telecommunication technologies and their effect on digital access, jobs, and mass communication for the digital welfare of all Americans.

  • ITS: The Nation’s Spectrum and Communications Lab:

  • The research and development branch of the NTIA. “The mission of ITS is to advance innovation in communications technologies, inform spectrum and communications policy for the benefit of all stakeholders, and investigate our nations most pressing telecommunications challenges through research that employees are proud to deliver.”

  • Office of Public Affairs:

  • The outward facing branch of NTIA that provides transparency and information to the public through the news media.

  • Office of Congressional Affairs:

  • Responsible for conveying information to elected Federal state, and local officials and lawmakers concerning the NTIA’s actions and policies and works with these officials in order to pass legislation.

  • Office of the Chief Counsel:

  • The legal branch of the NTIA that provides counsel to other offices within the NTIA as well as outside organizations to clarify “powers, duties, and responsibilities of the agency.” Also coordinates with the Office of Congressional Affairs to maintain and uphold the working scope of the NTIA within the power of the law.

  • Office of Public Safety Communications:

  • Supervises the duties of the NTIA in working with federal, state, local, and tribal communities to ensure the system of the nationwide public safety broadband network, known as FirstNet, is regularly serviced and maintains an operational status.

  • Office of Policy Coordination and Management:

  • The administrative branch of NTIA. This office develops and decides on all monetary functions of the agency, to include: submitting budgets, approving spending, and managing resource related programs, as well as acquiring IT management and staff.

  • Office of Intergovernmental Affairs:

  • Coordinates with other governmental entities and officials to broaden the availability and access to the Internet throughout all communities in the US.
